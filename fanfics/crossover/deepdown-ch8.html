spiked, black leather arm bands, and her most treasured pair of black 
leather knee-high boots which were adorned with countless silver buckles.  
People often referred to her as a witch or a vampire-wannabe, just thinking

preference.  But she realized that her persona was her best intimidation.  
After all, who wants to get in a row with someone who would place a curse on 
you or would try to bite your neck?  This is not to say that her persona 
worked on everyone—just most.  At the moment, though, Hotaru knew she was 
going to have to use every piece of arsenal in her artillery to get back 
that folder, at whatever costs.

of her slipped one of the pictures out of the folder, and quickly handed 
everything back but the picture she had in her hand.  The teen could only 
blink as she felt her folder back in her hands, sans one photo.

serious, "In the end, they're just pictures that are worth no more than the 
cost to print them."  Emerald eyes danced as they looked upon the Goth in 
front of her.

too late...'  Purple eyes closed and the small girl clutched at her folder, 
gasping for air from holding her breath for so long.  When finally Hotaru 
opened her eyes, she saw the picture that was in the taller girls hands 
being held in front of her face.

	"This is one of your better ones, in my humble opinion."

	The pale girl could only stare at the photograph being held in front of 
her.  She felt as though she'd forgotten how to speak.  'How did she?... 
When?...' Her small frame shrugged over in obvious defeat and Hotaru reached 
up, taking the picture from the brute.  Dark purple eyes looked up to meet a 
definably softer gaze from her peer, a smile crossing the shorter girls face 
as she stared.

	Makoto had decided, during the teen Goth's obvious agony, to sit on the top 
of the desk behind her, bringing her legs up and crossing them underneath 
her.  She now had her head in her hands and was giving Hotaru a very pitying 
look.

	"Do you even take photographs yourself?"

	"Nah, I'm not really into all that snazzy art stuff."  The tomboy chuckled 
in her own amusement and watched discreetly as the purple-eyed girl packed 
up her things.

choice of picture proves you definitely have no taste."  Hotaru smirked.  
"And that you're far too intense for you're own good."

by the sound of an escaped laugh.  Quickly the chestnut haired girl brought 
a chapped hand up and covered her mouth, laughing several more times in the 
process.  The dark haired girl watched and couldn't help but genuinely smile 
at the scene.

	A crash from outside startled both girls in the empty classroom.  Two sets 
of eyes shot towards the closed door and in no less than a second, both 
girls were making their way towards it, giving one another curious glances 
along the way.

	7, September 2005
Begin Entry: 20:45

	I was finally able to return to school today, with my mother's blessing 
that is.  The past several days were probably more tiring on my body than if 
I was actually at school; at least that's what I'd like to believe.  On 
Monday, after Mother left for work, I pulled out my calculus book and read 
the next six chapters.  Having been watched like a hawk by Mother for the 
entire rest of the Sunday, I did not get a chance to do my homework.  Just 
to be on the safe side, I also did the work for each of those chapters, 
triple checking my answers.

It was around 13:00 by the time I finished and I decided to prepare a 
sandwich for lunch.  During lunch, my thoughts couldn't help but wander 
towards Kino Makoto.  Though I've recorded bits and pieces of what I could 
find out about the 'Rogue', I'd never actually spoken to her until Sunday.  
My feelings for this girl have been swaying like a pendulum from the moment 
I first greeted her.

The day at the hospital, I felt, did not go well at all with her.  Her green 
eyes are far too intense, always cutting through me like a heated dagger 
through butter.  And though it may just be my imagination, I believe she 
wanted me to feel like I did; her first two weeks at the school were filled 
with nothing but intimidation tactics so that people would stay away.  
Everything that I experienced was exactly what I had expected from her, with 
the exception of the short time just after my panic attack.

	There was a definitive difference in that Kino Makoto than in any I'd had 
observed.  Though the experience was aggravating and tiresome, in the end, I 
felt calmer than I had been all day.  Could it be that she intended for 
things to turn out that way?  This 'Rogue' is either very people-savvy or 
just plain lucky.

	After lunch I intended to clear my head of that scraggly and bothersome 
annoyance by reading two of the assigned books in my American Literature 
class.  At around 16:00, Minako called my house to see how I was doing.  We 
chatted for a bit about nothing in particular and when I hung up with her no 
less than 15 minutes later, Usagi called with the same questions as Minako.  
I am happy to see my friends care for me so much.

Rei called later that evening, but our conversation was cut short as she had 
priestess duties to attend to.  When my mother arrived back home at 21:27, I 
had finished both books.  For the life of me, however, I could not decipher 
any of what Hemingway was trying to say in The Sun Also Rises.  I made a 
note in my planner to reread the book over the weekend.

	Mother scolded me for not having had dinner and proceeded to call a 
take-out place.  It was true, I was hungry, but I'd felt if I stopped 
reading my thoughts would once again return to dancing green eyes.

	The following day, I finished work in Advanced Science and History for the 
next several chapters.  The night before I'd planned to keep myself busy all 
day and prepared my food in advance so that I wouldn't have any time in 
letting my mind wander.  Rei came over after school and gave me the homework 
for the past two days.  We talked about what had happened and I thanked her 
for being genuinely concerned, but there was no need for her to worry.

	Rei is such a good friend.

	I finished the work with time to spare that evening.  Feeling that I was 
ready and prepared, I went to sleep early.  I dreamt that I was flying 
through the air, awkwardly.  I couldn't see where I was going and kept 
bumping into things.  When I bumped into a window, I looked closely at the 
reflection and saw that I was a bat.  I woke up at that point to the alarm.

	I arrived at school at 07:10, as always, and prepared for the day through 
my planner.  Everyone began to arrive in the classroom 20 minutes later 
along with Rei, with a brown haired underclassman clinging to her.  The two 
said their goodbyes at the door and Rei sat in the desk next to me, 
complaining that she had to tutor this girl in history.  From the looks of 
the two when they first walked in, I don't think Rei really minded that 
much.

	As the bell rang, the 'Rogue' walked in.  Her green eyes were dancing again 
and as she turned down the row, she caught my eyes and nodded, acknowledging 
me.  I couldn't help the blush that crept across my face.  The teacher began 
to speak and I was lucky enough to have a distraction.  But it didn't help 
because during the entire class, I felt her drilling into the back of my 
head with those eyes.  Why does she make me feel this way?

	After class, Rei pulled me quickly out of the room, much to my dismay.  I 
had decided to stick around in hopes to be able to speak to Makoto.  I 
thought that if I just spoke to her, I could get those feelings to dissolve 
quickly and I wouldn't be so sidetracked by them.  She would remind me of 
the uncouth brute she actually is and I could go back to disliking her.

	At our lockers, Rei was saying something to me, but I can't remember what 
it was.  My next class was calculus and started at 09:00, so I had to say my 
farewells to her and depart down the hall.  Calculus went much smoother than 
American Literature.  I spent most of the period helping Michiru and Haruka 
understand the new calculations and how to apply them to the problems on the 
board.  Those two are so enigmatic, I believe one can't help but be cheerful 
when around them.  Even Advanced Science, the next class, wasn't so bad, 
despite having felt those emerald eyes on me the entire time.

	Morning classes having finished at 12:25, I was heading off to lunch with 
Minako and Usagi on my tail.  They, of course, both have different classes 
than Rei and I in the morning, and so they're always waiting for us after 
Advanced Science.  Rei stayed behind, as she still had to finish up the 
experiment, and told us to go on ahead.  I looked around quickly for Makoto, 
but she had already left as well.

As we walked down the hall, Usagi had her arm around my shoulder probing as 
to whether or not I was actually okay, though I repeatedly told her I was 
fine.  Minako was to the right of me saying that if she'd had a panic 
attack, she'd probably milk the after effects for a couple of weeks if only 
to get out of doing any school work.

	I couldn't help but smile.

	It was then we rounded the corner and who could be standing there?  It was 
none other than Kino Makoto.  She was leaning against her locker, chestnut 
hair falling across her eyes and parts of her face, looking positively 
comfortable and even, somewhat, good looking.  The school requires that all 
students wear uniforms but there is a flaw in the charter: it does not 
specify that girls should wear girl uniforms and boys their own.  I have 
only ever known Haruka to cross that boundary; until Makoto came, that is.

	She wore her uniform well though, I'll admit.  Juuban is very relaxed about 
how the students wear their uniforms, as long as they wear them.  The slacks 
were a tad on the baggy side; her shirt was not tucked in and was left 
unbuttoned, revealing a black t-shirt underneath.  Despite her overall 
grunginess, she reminded me of one of the boys I would often see 
skateboarding in the park sometimes.  Surprisingly, I found myself attracted 
and repulsed at the same time.

	I didn't realize that I had stopped in the middle of the hall with both 
Usagi and Minako giving me awry glances for staring.  It was several seconds 
before Minako touched my hand, bringing me back to life.  She whispered in 
my ear that she thought Makoto was really cute and that she thought 'The New 
Transfer' and I would make a doubly cute couple.  I could only blush.

	Usagi giggled unabashedly and before I knew it, she had trotted over to the 
'Rogue'.  My body had paralyzed itself as I watched 'Meatball head' chatting 
animatedly with Makoto and I felt a rush of feelings swarm through my 
stomach up my throat.  It was jealousy clear and simple.  Makoto was smiling 
and nodding as Usagi talked to her.  I wondered why she couldn't be nice 
like that with me all the time.

	Minako, meanwhile, had covertly managed to get me to move my feet in the 
direction of the 'Rogue' and when I'd noticed what she was doing, I was 
already half way there.  I gave her a look that said I didn't want to go and 
she smiled in response,  trying to encourage me by saying that it would be 
okay.  For some reason, my throat had dried out completely, and I couldn't 
explain why I didn't want to go.

	I'll explain it here: It came to me during the end of Calculus that Kino 
Makoto was the type of person you did not approach.  She approached you, but 
the rest of time, she wished to be left alone.  Or, at least, that's what I 
came up with in my calculations.

	I met her emerald-like eyes as I approached and instantly I became 
mesmerized.  All logic left my brain as I stood in front of her, my mouth 
partly open and I believe, blushing madly.  She simply watched me.  She 
didn't say hello, she didn't ask how I was, nor did she even try to make 
conversation about some silly topic.  At first I thought perhaps she was 
feeling as I was, but when I studied her face, she wasn't at all tense and 
looked slightly bored.

	That hurt me, a lot.  I managed to find my voice and greeted her, asking 
how she was doing today.  I didn't get the chance to get her response 
however, as I felt a strong hand grab my arm and pull me away quickly.  It 
startled me so I yelped out, turning to find that my attacker was actually 
Rei pulling me to lunch.  She said we had to hurry because she planned to 
meet up with Tatsumi, the underclassman she agreed to tutor, at the end of 
lunch to discuss their schedule.

	The rest of the day went by in a blur; I had cram school after regular 
school and did not arrive home until 18:00.  After a light dinner, I spent 
the next few hours studying.  But I still cannot concentrate.  In writing 
this, I hope to pinpoint the problem in my lack of concentration efforts and 
fix it.  There is nothing left to say at the moment, but I have a feeling 
that this semester is just beginning.
