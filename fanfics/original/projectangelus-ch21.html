      An awkward silence followed after Abby uttered the dead name of her husband. He 
looked the exact as he had the morning he left the house, briefcase and lunch in hand. He 
was supposed to come back a little earlier from work to go buy some things for Eveon's 
upcoming birthday...

      He didn't even make it to work when his car was smashed into an oncoming lane. Abby 
took  an unsure step towards him, doubting the whole entire situation. Sure, the Hall of 
Memories summoned dead people back, but could it really be Terry in front of her?

      He shifted his weight to one foot before fiddling with his hair. "This wasn't exactly 
how I wanted us to meet up again... I was hoping..." He looked up at the black mass that 
contained Sakura. "I was hoping it would be under less tense circumstances..."

      More silence followed until he turned his bright blue eyes back at her. "How's 
Eveon?"

      Abby couldn't help herself. She took the last few steps toward him before launching 
into his arms. He barely caught her, just as surprised at her reaction. He was very warm - 
nothing like the malignant form of Diana.

      She started to cry and he held her closely, trying to calm her. It was all oddly 
nostalgic to be with him so she cried even harder. "I'm so sorry," Terry murmured. "I 
didn't mean to leave you all alone..."

      "But you did...!" she sobbed out. "How was I supposed to...? How did you expect me to 
go on?"

      He was silent as she began to calm down, still nestled against him. "I had to explain 
it all to Eveon," Abby whispered. "Why you couldn't be there for any more of her 
birthdays..."

      "I know," he said, closing his eyes. "You had to do a lot without me and I just 
wished... that I didn't have to leave both of you."

      He wiped away her tears and said, "I missed you."

      "I... I missed you too," Abby whispered.

      He suddenly laughed. "I guess a lot of things have changed since I left, huh?"

      Abby flushed bright red and sighed. "God, this is really what you're here for isn't 
it?"

      "No, no," Terry said, shaking his head. "I'm not a master of scathing jokes like you 
are. Bringing it up seems to be enough to embarrass you. I'm just glad Eveon's happy, 
but..."

      He looked back over at the black mass. "I'm sure you know why I'm really here."

      Abby's heart began to race... it almost felt like something was squeezing her chest. 
"Y-yeah..."

      He rubbed her shoulder gently as he continued talking. "I'm glad that you've found 
someone since I've been gone... I'd rather have that than have you mourn over me all the 
time-"

      Abby cut him off. "You want me to choose, don't you?"

      He shrugged sadly. "Yeah... I'm sorry to do this to you... to make you decide between 
me or her, but you've been thinking about it for a long time. The moment you first started 
to fall for the Living Inferno, you began to ask that question. Who do you love more?"

      "I just... Do I really have to do this?!" Abby wrenched herself away from him. "This 
is cruel to have to choose! This isn't black or white or... or... I can't..."

      "Do you really love her? Or is she something to comfort you because I'm gone? If I 
were still alive... would you have ever fallen in love with her? Would you still be with 
me?" He paused with a smile. "But you already know the answer to that, don't you?"

      "Terry-" Abby stopped as the world around them began to shift and change.

      "I'm... I'm kind of glad I died," he said suddenly. "It makes this more easier on 
me..." His tone began to change to sadness though he kept smiling. "Call me prideful, but I 
think I can rest easier knowing that my wife hasn't been stolen from me while I was 
around."

      Abby felt something shudder through her. "I'm sorry," she whispered. "I can't... I 
can't leave her."

      "I know," Terry said with a grin. "But you just needed a little shove to assure you 
of that. Even if I hadn?t died, I still would have lost you... you just can't seem to let 
her go."

      "I still... I still love you," she said, almost trying to patch up something that was 
crumbling.

      Terry laughed as he started to disappear. "You love me... but just not as much." He 
winked at her. "I'll always love you."

      Abby's eyes began to tear up as she realized what had just happened. Her husband... 
the man she'd wanted see for so long was gone again. She sunk to her knees as she began to 
cry again, upset but relieved. She loved Sakura... so much that it hurt whenever she 
thought of Terry. It had always been so heart rending and now it was all over...

      Marx walked slowly over to her, but she lashed out at him. "Couldn't you do something 
about this?"

disappeared. "It shapes itself based upon what is needed. And what was needed... was your 
pain."

on the ground. "Sakura!"

the Living Inferno. Abby gently lifted Sakura's head to rest on her lap as piercing red

back to something she didn't want.

      "It's alright," a voice rasped out. "I will... I will be alright as long as..."

turned to look at Marx. "What's wrong with her?!"

      "She has to transcend the Living Inferno," Marx said, putting his hands in his 
pockets as the armor burst into flames. "This is the easy part."

      "I know you like them," Sakura said quietly. "But I couldn't-"

      "It's fine," Abby said with a slight laugh. "I love you no matter you look like."

headed. The way Sakura was looking at her... the Living Inferno kissed her fervently, 
making her intentions known. She'd gone a long time not touching Abby, since her hellish 
rebirth in Purgatory, so...

      Marx coughed loudly. "May I suggest not doing that in the Hall of Memories? While I 
sure would enjoy watching, as would Terry if he's still lingering around, I think I should 
take you back to the hotel room and then make myself scarce. Deal?"

      Sakura didn't like being interrupted, but Marx was right... "Okay," Abby sighed, 
holding Sakura closer. "I guess it's time to go back."

      Sakura leaned against Abby, enjoying the contact. "Yes... then we can worry about 
everything else..."
