"Pass the onions, will you?"

"Hai." So, you decided to have Japanese tonight, miso, in fact. I take 
it you got bored of French?

"Lettuce."

"Hai."

"Mushrooms."

I hand over the mushrooms and snicker as you try to read the direction, 
written in Japanese, on the packages. "Here." I snatch and read them 
with ease, then prepared the soup base. I go set the table while you 
finish chopping the vegetables.

In a few minutes dinner's ready. There's this quietness as we eat. It's 
not uncomfortable; it's rather peaceful....Mireille? Mireille, are you 
having trouble with your chopsticks?!

"What? Is there something on my teeth?" No, it's just you can't use 
chopsticks.

"Here, I'll help you," I say as I get up and put my arms around you. 
"You hold it like a pencil. Like this." I put your fingers where they 
should be. "Wha...no, no, like *this*." I reposition them. "Now move them 
like this." I can't believe you're having a hard time with this; it's 
so easy.


Afterwards, we drink tea. Not green tea; I know how much you hate it. 
Do you remember when I disappeared while you were shopping? I was out 
buying Darjeeling. I know how much you like it. It feels cozy...sitting 
here, sipping tea, your hand laced with mine.

"Kirika?"

"Hmm?"

"I'm thinking of opening a tea shop." Where did that come from? "I'm 
getting bored. Hits aren't coming in anymore, we're not otherwise 
employed, and shops are getting old."

"So you'll sell tea."

"I take it you'll join me?"

"Hai. If you let me stock green tea."

"Anything you want. We'll even stock purple tea if you like."

I snicker and shake my head slowly. There's no such thing as purple 
tea.
