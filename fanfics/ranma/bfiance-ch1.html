"Nope!" Ranma said proudly. "Ain't got no use for it runnin' a
dojo."

"Um, I'm not sure that's, er, accurate."

"Huh?"

"You should at least have your high school diploma, Ranma."

"Hmmm... I dunno... Pops always said I didn't really need school."

"Did _he_ finish school?"

"Um, I dunno." Ranma stood on her knees and leaned over the back of
the couch. "Yo, Pops!"

The panda on one side of the shogi board looked up.

"Did ya ever finish high school?"

The panda held up a sign. "Of course! What do you think I am?"
~~Flip.~~ "Stupid?"

With a sour face, Ranma said, "Don't push the issue, Pops." She
flopped back down next to Akane. "Well, so much for preachin' what
he's practiced. School, huh? I dunno..."

"You should finish high school, Ranma. I think you might regret it
if you don't."

"Yeah... Oh, damn! Well, that's out."

"What?"

"This curse, Akane! Cold water hunts me down! I bet I wouldn't even
make it ta class the first day without gettin' wet! Oh, well. So
much for that idea." Ranma didn't sound terribly disappointed.

Akane tapped her finger against her lips. "Cold water finds you,
hmmm? Well, there IS a solution to that problem..."

Ranma flinched after looking into Akane's calculating eyes. "Uh...
Do I wanna know?"

Akane just smiled broadly, not comforting Ranma one iota.

                        -----=====-----

"Where do you think you're going?!"

Ranma blinked at the door to the room she shared with her father.
"Um... to bed?"

"Right. But not in there!"

Scratching the top of her head, Ranma said, "Um, ah, er... Where?"

Akane sighed dramatically, took Ranma by the arm, and dragged her
into a bedroom with a wooden-duck nameplate. The "Akane" banged
against the door as it slammed shut.

"Ranma no baka..." filtered through the wood in an affectionate
tone.

                        -----=====-----

Clutching handfuls of her blanket, Nabiki lay rigid in her bead,
sweating heavily, drops of salty water rolling down the sides of her
face.

A soft knock preceded the opening of the door, and Kasumi wafted in,
shutting the door behind her.

"Are you all right, Nabiki?"

"I'm... not feeling so hot... or rather... too hot... um..."

Kasumi tsked, and sat down on the bed, leaned over, and kissed
Nabiki on the lips. She withdrew, smiling naughtily. "You'll feel
better after a little exercise."

Nabiki's eyes widened. "You... You fed me something!"

"Mmm-hmmm. Mother knew more than just how to cook; you should really
read her cookbooks sometime. It's very educational. At any rate,
Akane's going to be busy from now on, and I'm not of a mind to do
without, so..."

"But there's Tofu-sensei!"

"I know, but... a desire for variety runs in the family, I'm
beginning to think, and this _keeps_ it in the family."

Nabiki just stared up at her, trembling. Kasumi sighed and said,
"I'm sorry, Nabiki; I don't know what I was thinking. I can fix you
a remedy. I won't do this to you. Akane's a bad influence on me,
perhaps."

She made to rise, but Nabiki caught her arm with a shaking hand.
"No... S-Stay."

"Are... Are you sure? It's not just the herbs talking?"

"I... I th-think so. B-But... I d-don't want t-to b-be alone."

Smiling, Kasumi pulled the metal cord underneath the shade on
Nabiki's bedside lamp, draping the room in darkness. The
conversation now consisted of soft breathing and the sounds of
kissing. Covers and clothing rustled, and Nabiki cried out in soft
passion.

                        -----=====-----

Soun lay on his futon, looking up at the dark ceiling.

"A girl curse, of all things. Heh, that Saotome's so clever,
covering all the bases."

                        -----=====-----

Genma-panda sneezed, and then groaned. Without his pre-bed razzing
of the boy, Genma felt... lonely. There wasn't anyone to gripe about
his snoring. He sighed.

He could still hear who he assumed to be Ranma and Akane calling to
each other in the midst of the kami knew what. He abruptly sat up in
bed.

*Nodoka! Oh, no! ... Well, there's nothing for it.*

Rising to his feet, Genma padded over to the light switch and
flipped it up. He dragged his pack out of the closet and rummaged
around, eventually pulling a metal liquor flask into the light. He
shook it, listening to the liquid slosh around.

He'd intended holding this over Ranma's head if the boy gave any
trouble over the engagement. Now, it looked as if he'd need it for a
very different reason. A mental image of girl Ranma being cradled by
Akane flashed through his mind, and the panda shivered.

*And only enough for one. Still, katana-- ERRR, honor comes first.*
