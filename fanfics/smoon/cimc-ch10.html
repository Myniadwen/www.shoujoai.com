Instead, she collapsed on the hospital bed with a slight fwump,

"I found you in the ruins of that place," Ami said, brushing her hair

"Ugh...the Chief? What did he say? And ruins? What?"  Rei opened her

Hey,
 
Glad you didn’t die, but consider yourself on paid vacation.  The

Love you(I guess),

Rei fell back on the bed.  

"Dammit."  Ami shook her head and looked Rei over.

"What happened to you?"

"Serenity happened to me.  I got there, to her um, ‘chamber,’" she saw

"Bit you?"

"Yeah.  I got mad; she completely broke down.  It was...weird.

"This is bad..."  Ami said, paling immediately.  She remembered how

"What?"

"Well...you are...you do have bite marks.  You’re probably going

Mitsuki was bored.  She had been waiting for the past two hours for this 
detective, and there was no one in sight.  Usually, a few curious kids, 
or a weapon collector would make their way in here, but it was oddly 
quiet.  Her employer had been behaving oddly for the past three weeks 
before her odd request the night before.  And then there was Minako.  
Mitsuki had no idea where she was either, and she was worried.  The 
woman was odd, but she seemed kind enough.  And she had rescued her; it 
didn’t seem right that she would leave without some sort of reward.
 
She yawned and blew dust off of the counter, looking toward the back 
room.  Would it hurt to check before the detective arrived?  

What Kazuki doesn’t know won’t hurt her, Mitsuki thought, making her way 
toward the room.  Kazuki always said she needed to study things that 
were not always obvious at first glance; this was the perfect 
opportunity to do so.  

She knocked on the wall near the door three times as instructed, and she 
heard a noise from behind it.  She turned the knob of the door and 
slipped into the dusty, dark room, fumbling around for a light.

"She should have added, "And do bring a flashlight, dear," because it is 
seriously dark in here,"  Mitsuki grumbled, moving to pull a cobweb off 
of her shoulder.  She finally found a light switch and flicked it on.  
As Kazuki had stated, there was a book with a key in the center of the 
room, on an old wood table.

Mitsuki lifted the book and blew dust off of the cover.  She grabbed the 
key and put it into a barely noticeable latch on the book, taking great 
care not to destroy the pages.  She recognized Kazuki’s handwriting 
immediately.

December 2, 1890

Today, Father allowed me to play in the square, and I saw a rather odd 
young woman.  She was dressed as if she came from a wealthy family, and 
I wondered why she would ever waste her time in a place like this.  This 
is a small town, and a rich woman would not waste her time watching 
someone such as myself play.  She had a very interesting skin tone, 
perhaps she was somewhere from the islands far off?  Whatever the case, 
she seemed oddly interested in me.  I could not see her very well from 
were I had been sitting, but I could...feel her eyes on me, it was all 
rather strange.  The woman walked over to me and asked me what my name 
was.  I told her, and she said that her name was Setsuna Meiou.  I was 
almost sure she was from somewhere far off now, as her name was foreign.  
She said she had traveled a ‘long way’ to meet someone like me, I wonder 
what she meant?  She was quite strange, but she said she could help me 
with my piano.  I want to be great, world-famous.  It is quite rare to 
have women achieve things in this day and age; I’d like to trust this 
woman, but I am still confused.  Why would she choose me, and why had 
she been traveling all this time to meet me?

"18...90?"  Mitsuki whispered, staring at the weathered pages.

"How...how old are you anyway, Kazuki?"
