"Corel...Corel..." Yuffie bit her lip, squinted her eyes. "Hey! What 
rhymes with Corel?" 

Tifa spared a glance at the bouncy ninja, and shrugged lightly, frowning 
in thought. 

"Bell?" 

"Shell?" Aeris offered. 

Yuffie scowled, kicked at the dust on the road, bit her lip once more 
before she spoke. 

"No way...that's not gonna work. See, I'm thinking, with my songs..." 

Tifa looked heavenward. "Again?" she mumbled. Yuffie ignored her, fell 
into conversation once more. 

"See, I'm trying to...think of a new creative vision." She waved her 
hands dramatically, spun like a ballerina. "Like, I'm trying to take 
them in a new direction!" 

Aeris smiled serenely, stared at Tifa, a comfortable, knowing gaze. "I 
bet Tifa knows how to sing." 

Yuffie guffawed loudly, slapping her hands against her knees and falling 
to the ground in a melodramatic demonstration of amusement. "Gawd, no 
way, Aeris! Now, I have to say...Tifa's a pretty decent fighter...I 
mean, not better than me, but you know," she smirked, "not many people 
are. But singing? Nope, no way, I bet she can't. 'Sides, even if she 
could, she wouldn't." The spry young woman hopped back up to her feet in 
an instant. "She doesn't like sharing with us, you know." There was 
something hidden in her tone. 

Tifa opened her mouth to say something, but Aeris shook her head, gave 
Yuffie an exasperated grin. "I'm sure Tifa can sing." She turned to the 
brunette, waiting for her to speak. "Can you?" 

"My mother was a better singer..." Tifa said softly. "Besides...I 
haven't done it in a long time...since she um..." she trailed off 
awkwardly. "I just don't really sing anymore," she finished quickly. 

Yuffie frowned; Aeris lowered her head and kicked at the ground. 

"Sorry," the flower girl murmured. "Your mother was a very wonderful 
woman, Tifa." 

"Yeah, I..." the martial artist paused, turned toward Aeris and blinked, 
a befuddled expression on her face. "Wait...how do you..." 

Aeris lifted her hand, shook her head. Nothing more needed to be said. 
Tifa walked ahead of the other two, her eyes strange and distant. 

Yuffie cringed. She had that...feeling again. That odd tingling in the 
back of her head, a whisper of something she couldn't place. It made her 
nervous. She didn't like being nervous. Being a thief was all about 
keeping your hands steady, being alert. Being a ninja was very much the 
same. Suddenly, Yuffie felt as if she was somehow being excluded from 
something. She decided she didn't like that either. No one kept Yuffie 
Kisaragi out of anything. She was going to find out what this was all 
about. The Gold Saucer trip would be a perfect time to figure out this 
little mystery. She giggled deviously, rubbed her hands together. 

Time to do some detective work. 

~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~ 

Corel was a little scrap of nowhere in the world, a dusty town full of 
dilapidated buildings, broken glass and twisted steel. The people, 
driven by despair and anger, lived off selling what they could, trying 
in vain to get enough money from passerbys on the way to the Gold 
Saucer. Those who could left the place as soon as they were able, their 
minds twisted by the stories they had heard, their hearts hardened 
against the one man whom they believed was at fault. 

Barret Wallace. 

Pulling her thoughts away from her own problems, Tifa noticed Barret's 
stiff way of walking, his hunched shoulders as he reached the town. 
Barret had told her some things, but not all, and now, with morbid 
curiousity, she and the others walked into the once-prestigious coal 
town, another hapless victim of Shinra and Mako. 

~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~ 

"Stay here," Barret said to Cloud and the others when they reached the 
town. He approached a tired, pale man, said something inaudible. Tifa 
watched carefully, clenched her fists at her sides. Something was about 
to happen. 

The man stepped foward, and pulled his fist back, punching Barret in the 
stomach. Barret slumped slightly, but he did not retaliate, only stared 
at the man sadly. Tifa gasped, moved to intervene, but Aeris gripped her 
arm before she could. She struggled; Aeris frowned, pulled her closer. 

"Don't," she whispered. "Let Barret handle it." Tifa shook a little, 
waited for Aeris to loosen her grip before she relaxed. 

"Well, lookey here! Never thought I'd see your face again!" the man 
said, spitting at the ground near Barret's feet. "They kick you out of 
another town or somethin'?" Barret didn't answer; the man made a 
disgusted noise in the back of his throat. 

"You destroy everything you touch," he growled, stepping back. Other men 
gathered around the first, all staring at Barret with the same cold, 
angry eyes. Tifa shuddered. It made her uneasy; it made her worry. 
Barret never took such abuse from anyone. If someone so much as stared 
at him the wrong way, he would beat them to a pulp. Why wasn't he 
fighting back? 

"You got a lot of balls comin' back here! Look at this place! It's all 
your fault North Corel turned into a garbage heap!" another man spat 
angrily. Barret sighed, looked at his feet. 

"...I'm sorry..." he all but whispered. Tifa moved again; Aeris held 
fast to her. 

"Look what they're doing to him!" Tifa whispered in a low hiss. "Let me 
go..." 

"No," Aeris whispered back. "Do you really think he wants you involved, 
or any of us? Stay still and wait." 

Tifa mumbled something unintelligable, brooded silently, but she did as 
Aeris asked. 

The first man let out a string of curses, turned away from Barret. 

"You ain't even worth the effort. Don't waste your time talkin' to the 
Techno-freak!" The others dispersed, Barret watched them go, slumped his 
massive shoulders, sighed again. He returned to the group, eyes still 
downcast. 

"You heard 'em..." he muttered gruffly. "It's my fault this town 
was...destroyed..." 

Tifa stared at him sadly, but before she could speak, he ran off. 

Cloud shook his head, looked around at the small, dingy town before 
turning to the others. 

"We better follow him. Looks like he headed to the Ropeway. Come on." 

~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~ 

The others somberly made their way to the Ropeway station; Tifa leaned 
against the wall, waited for someone to say something. She wasn't sure 
she trusted her voice. Aeris spoke first, moved toward Barret. 

"Barret, what happened?" she murmured quietly. Barret stared at her 
sadly, shook his head. 

"Sorry." 

Cloud stepped up, stared carefully at Barret. "What happened?" 

"My hometown used to be around here." 

Yuffie and Red XIII shared confused looks; Red shrugged lightly, cleared 
his throat. 

"What do you mean, 'used to'?" he asked Barret. Barret turned to the 
creature, sighed again. 

"It ain't here no more. Heard it got buried...in just four years." 

"But how could those people say such terrible things?" Aeris asked. 
Barret didn't seem to hear her. 

"An' it's my fault. ALL my fault." His voice almost seemed to tremble; 
Tifa looked away. It was strange to see him like this, to see him so 
sad, so tired. 

"Corel was always a coal mining town," he began. "It's so dusty, but 
calm and so poor...a real small town, this one. That's the first time I 
ever heard the word "Mako reactor" mentioned since that time..." 

As Barret explained what had happened to Corel, how his wife had died, 
his family, how Shinra had destroyed everything he ever loved, Tifa felt 
her mind wander to a place, years ago. She saw herself, younger, her 
body shaking with broken sobs. Screaming for revenge, begging for her 
father to wake up. All because of Shinra. They ruined so many lives... 

"...but more than Shinra, I couldn't forgive myself," she heard Barret 
saying. "Never should have gone along with the building of the reactor." 
Tifa blinked, wiped away a tear. 

"Don't blame yourself," she whispered. "We were all fooled by the 
promises Shinra made back then." 

Yuffie looked down, sniffled slightly. Red XIII shook out his mane, 
looked at the ground. Aeris shifted, moved toward Tifa, laced their 
fingers together. Tifa looked over at her, smiled. 

~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~ 

Aeris was more excited than anyone else to see the Gold Saucer. 

"I've never been," she whispered breathlessly to Tifa when she tried to 
get her to calm down. "It's so beautiful." 

"I've never been either." Tifa smiled. "It's great...so many lights. 
I've never seen so many lights before...but still," she shook her head, 
"remember, we're not really here to goof off, okay?" 

Aeris pouted, tugged Tifa into the entrance of the massive theme park. 
"But just because we aren't here to goof off doesn't mean that we 
shouldn't." She winked. "Let's go!" 

By the time they walked in, the others were there already. Yuffie was a 
ball of energy, buzzing this way and that. 

"Gawd, did you see the outside! All of those lights and the Gondola and 
the roller coasters and the big chocobo and stuff!" 

Aeris grinned, glad to find someone on her wavelength. 

"Let's have fun!" 

She stared over at Barret, who was facing the wall. Tifa winced, tried 
to grab Aeris before she could say anything, but it was too late. 

"I know this isn't the right time to do this...hey Barret, cheer up!" 

Barret stepped away, glared at her. 

"I ain't in no cheery mood. So jes' leave me alone." Aeris shrugged, 
seeming unconcerned. 

"Really? That's too bad." She ran over to Cloud, hopped up and down. 

"Let's go!" Tifa walked over to Aeris, grabbed her shoulder, pulled her 
away. 

"Wasn't that a little harsh, Aeris?!" she whispered. 

"Just act normal when this happens," Aeris whispered back. 

"You think so...?" Tifa asked uncertainly. 

"Of course!" Aeris ran over to Barret, tugging Tifa behind her. "We're 
gonna go play..." 

Tifa looked apologetic, pleaded with Barret with her eyes, but he didn't 
seem to notice. He growled low, waved his arms in the air angrily. 

"So PLAY!...messin' round #@^%! Don't forget we're after Sephiroth!" He 
hopped into one of portals, disappeared. Aeris sighed. 

"...I think he's mad." 

Tifa shook her head. Still the same Aeris. 

~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~*~~ 

"The Speed Square?" Aeris groaned. She leafed through a tiny brouchure 
in her hand, rattled off options to Tifa. "Why couldn't we go to the 
Event Square and see a play? Or the Wonder Square? They have lots of 
games there. Or the Chocobo Square, or...we could have gone to the Round 
Square and rode the Gondola, and watched the lights and saw everything 
from way up high..." she sighed. "That would have been nice." 

Tifa scoffed. "The Speed Square is fun." 

"No it's not. You just ride around in a stupid rollercoaster and shoot 
things." 

"Then leave." Tifa challenged. 

"Fine." 

Tifa blinked. "You're leaving?" 

"Yep." 

"Seriously?" 

"Yes." 

"But...I'm going to be here by myself." 

"So?" She turned on her heel. "I'm going." 

Tifa sighed. Either stay alone and play the shooting game, or go and do 
something terribly uninteresting with Aeris. But it would be with Aeris. 
She stared longingly at the rollercoaster. 

"So..." she scratched her head, shuffled from side to side. "I'll see 
you around then," she grinned. 

Aeris blanched, shook her head vehemently, stomped her foot. "That's not 
fair! You're not supposed to do reverse...reverse psychology." 

"Well, I just did." Tifa hopped into one of the seats. "You coming?" 

Aeris bedrudgingly took a seat next to Tifa, rolled her eyes. "Next time 
we come here...we're doing something I want to do." 

Tifa smiled, stared at Aeris. The flower girl's face was flushed from 
annoyance, strands of hair fell out of place. Tifa brushed them back 
into place with her hand. 

"Okay." 

"You promise?" Aeris seemed uncertain. 

"I promise." She smiled, then blinked when she heard the familiar ring 
of her PHS. "Hello?" 

"Tifa." Cloud sounded...worried? "Something went wrong." 

"Wrong?" 

"It's Barret." 

end of chapter seven
